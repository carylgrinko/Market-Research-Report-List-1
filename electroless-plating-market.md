<p><p>Electroless plating, also known as autocatalytic plating, is a chemical process used to deposit a layer of metal onto a substrate without the use of external electrical power. This method relies on a redox reaction, where the metal ions in a solution are reduced and deposited onto the surface of the material. Electroless plating is widely used for its ability to provide uniform coatings, even on complex geometries, and is commonly applied in industries such as automotive, electronics, aerospace, and oil & gas. Popular metals used in this process include nickel, copper, and gold, offering properties like corrosion resistance, wear resistance, and improved conductivity.</p><p>The Electroless Plating Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14.6% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing demand from the electronics and automotive sectors, where electroless plating is used for components like printed circuit boards (PCBs) and engine parts. The rise in miniaturization of electronic devices and the need for durable, high-performance coatings are key factors propelling the market. Additionally, advancements in plating technologies and the development of environmentally friendly solutions are emerging trends. The Asia-Pacific region is anticipated to dominate the market due to rapid industrialization and the presence of major electronics manufacturers. Overall, the market is poised for significant expansion, supported by innovation and growing industrial applications.</p></p>
