<p><ul><li>Electrochemical</li><li>Photoionization Detectors (PID)</li><li>Solid-state or Metal–Oxide–Semiconductor (MOS)</li><li>Catalytic</li><li>Infrared</li><li>Laser</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Gas sensors and metering devices are essential for monitoring air quality and detecting hazardous gases. Electrochemical sensors measure gas concentration via chemical reactions. Photoionization Detectors (PID) detect volatile organic compounds through ionization. Solid-state or Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor (MOS) sensors function by changing resistance in the presence of gases. Catalytic sensors detect flammable gases by oxidizing them. Infrared sensors use light absorption for gas identification, while laser sensors provide enhanced sensitivity and accuracy. Other emerging technologies also contribute to this market.</p></p>
