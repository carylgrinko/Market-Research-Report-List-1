<p><p>The wine cork market consists of natural and synthetic cork types. Natural corks, sourced from the bark of cork oak trees, are prized for their ability to allow micro-oxygenation and maintain wine quality. However, they can sometimes introduce cork taint. Synthetic corks, made from materials like plastic or rubber, offer a consistent seal and eliminate cork taint risks but may not provide the same aging benefits. Both types cater to different consumer preferences and wine styles, influencing their market demand.</p></p>
