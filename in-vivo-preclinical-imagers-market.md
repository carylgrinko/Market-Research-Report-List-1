<p><p>The in vivo preclinical imagers market consists of several imaging modalities. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) provides detailed soft tissue visualization and functional information. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) allows for metabolic processes observation with radiotracers. Micro Computed Tomography (micro-CT) offers high-resolution 3D imaging for bone and small animal studies. Optical Imaging uses fluorescence and bioluminescence to visualize cellular dynamics in real-time. Other imaging techniques include ultrasound and single-photon emission computed tomography (SPECT), broadening research capabilities in preclinical settings.</p></p>
